About Me

I am a PhD student in Computer Science at the University of Colorado - Boulder. I am advised by Professor Qin (Christine) Lv and Professor Morteza Karimzadeh. I have a B.S. in Biomedical Engineering from the University of Texas at Austin, and have completed summer internships at Nokia Bell Labs in Cambridge, UK, Johns Hopkins Applied Physics Laboratory, and a startup called Stryd which makes wearable footpods for measuring biomechanics of runners. In addition to my research interests, I am passionate about the Denver Nuggets NBA team, music, ultrarunning on trails, cycling, soccer, and skiing! I am also a big dork and enjoy programming smart WiFi lights to sync up with music, as well as programming applications for my own Garmin watch.

Research Interests

My research is on systems data science including signal processing, data mining, and developing deep learning models with applications in mobile and wearable computing and Internet-of-Things (IoT). I am interested in working on applications in the environment, healthcare, fitness, and behavioral health and wellbeing.
